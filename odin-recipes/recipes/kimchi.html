Kimchi

Home

Description

Kimchi is a traditional Korean side dish made from fermented vegetables, primarily napa cabbage and Korean radishes, seasoned with various spices. It's known for its tangy flavor and health benefits.

Ingredients

  • 1 large napa cabbage
  • 1/4 cup sea salt
  • 4 cups water
  • 1 tablespoon grated ginger
  • 1 tablespoon minced garlic
  • 2 tablespoons Korean red pepper flakes (gochugaru)
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 2 green onions, chopped
  • 1/2 cup fish sauce (optional)

    • Steps

    1. Cut the napa cabbage into quarters and remove the core. Chop into bite-sized pieces.
    2. In a large bowl, dissolve sea salt in water. Soak the cabbage pieces in the saltwater for 2-3 hours, turning occasionally.
    3. Rinse the cabbage under cold water and drain well.
    4. In a separate bowl, mix ginger, garlic, gochugaru, sugar, and fish sauce to create a paste.
    5. Add the drained cabbage and green onions to the paste, mixing thoroughly to coat all pieces.
    6. Pack the kimchi mixture into a clean jar or container, pressing down to remove air pockets.
    7. Leave at room temperature for 1-2 days to ferment, then refrigerate.
    8. Enjoy your homemade kimchi as a side dish or ingredient in various dishes!
